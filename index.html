The Process

Three steps from repo to story

No account. No API key. No server. Just you and your git history.

01
Clone the repo
Clone Commit Canvas once. It lives in your toolbox forever — no install, no package manager, no accounts.
./run.sh /path/to/project
02
Watch it read your git
It reads your .git directory directly. No API calls, no network requests, no authentication. Your data never leaves your machine.
03
Get a story page
A single HTML file opens in any browser. Cinematic title reveal, animated timeline, activity ring, milestone cards, contributor ranking, and a shareable certificate.
story.html → screenshot → post
What's Inside

Not a stats dashboard.
A story page.

Every section is designed to tell part of your project's story — not display data.

Cinematic Hero
Title types itself in, a line draws beneath it, stats count up. The opening sequence sets the tone.
Story Arc Detection
"The Marathon", "The Climb", "The Sprint", "The Return". The tool understands what kind of project you have.
Animated Timeline
A film-strip timeline that draws itself as you scroll. Drag left and right. Every node has a story.
Turning Points
First commit, version releases, comeback moments, peak months. The milestones that shaped your project.
Activity Ring
A single SVG ring that captures your consistency pattern. Not a grid — an iconic visualization.
Ranked Contributors
Gold, silver, bronze badges. Animated progress bars. Every contributor, ranked by commits.
The Shareable Artifact
A proof-of-work certificate at the bottom of every story page. Screenshot it. Post it on LinkedIn. Share it anywhere. This is the artifact that makes the whole thing worth building.
The Narrative Engine

Every project has a shape

Commit Canvas doesn't just show stats — it understands what kind of journey you took.

The Marathon
Consistent work over a long time. Not flashy. Just showed up, day after day. The most underrated form of momentum.
The Climb
Started quiet, got louder. Real momentum building. When early commits become late commits by an order of magnitude.
The Sprint
Built in intense sessions. Deep work, compressed into focused bursts. Not consistent — but remarkable when it happens.
The Return
Projects die when makers stop. This one didn't. Six months gone and then — a commit. That means something.
The Foundation
Started strong, found its rhythm. A mature project that doesn't need to prove anything anymore — just keeps working.
Fresh Start
Just begun. Every great project starts here — one commit, one decision, one step into the unknown.
